Wellness / Diet Reviews

Diet Reviews

Plain-English, evidence-based reviews of popular diets — adapted for African food culture and Kenyan kitchens. Methodology inspired by Harvard T.H. Chan Nutrition Source.

Mediterranean

Highly recommended

Olive oil, legumes, fish, vegetables and whole grains. Strongest evidence base for cardiovascular and metabolic health.

✓ Lower CVD risk
✓ Sustainable long term
✓ Rich in fibre & omega-3
⚠ Fish access inland

DASH Diet

Highly recommended

Designed to lower blood pressure: fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy, low sodium.

✓ Reduces systolic BP
✓ Clinically validated
✓ Family-friendly
⚠ Sodium tracking

Plant-Based

Recommended

Mostly plants, minimal animal products. Strong for weight management and longevity when planned well.

✓ Lower LDL
✓ High fibre
⚠ Plan B12, iron, omega-3

Intermittent Fasting

Context-dependent

Time-restricted eating (16:8). Useful tool, not magic. Food quality still matters more than the clock.

✓ Simple structure
✓ May aid weight loss
⚠ Not for pregnancy/T1D

Ketogenic

Use with clinical oversight

Very high-fat, very low-carb. Therapeutic for epilepsy and select metabolic conditions; restrictive long-term.

✓ Rapid early weight loss
⚠ Hard to sustain
⚠ Lipid profile shifts

Carnivore

Not recommended

Animal foods only. Lacks fibre, polyphenols and vitamin C. No long-term evidence base.

⚠ No fibre
⚠ Cardiovascular risk

Reviews are educational only. Book a consult for a plan built around your medical history.

About

A healthier global future, engineered from African roots.

Mlo is the Swahili word for “a meal” — the everyday plate that shapes a life. MloHealth is the ecosystem built around it: clinical dietetics, sports performance, wellness education, meal preps and charity under one roof.
